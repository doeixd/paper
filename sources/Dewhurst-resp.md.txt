### 1. The Shared Setup
Both you and Dewhurst notice a natural bridge between two concepts:
*   **Information-Theoretic Causation:** Specifically, Erik Hoel’s finding that macro-states can possess more "effective information" (EI) than micro-states.
*   **Dennett’s Real Patterns:** The idea that macro-patterns exist if they compress data and allow for predictions that a bit-map wouldn't.

### 2. Dewhurst’s Deflationary Argument
Dewhurst looks at Hoel’s math and says: *“This is cool, but it doesn’t prove that macro-objects have new causal powers (ontological emergence). It only proves that macro-descriptions are easier for us to use (epistemic emergence).”*

Dewhurst relies on two standard metaphysical objections:
*   **Kim’s Causal Exclusion:** If the micro-state does all the physical work, the macro-state is causally redundant.
*   **"Fat-Handedness":** You can't actually intervene *only* on a macro-state. If you intervene on a brain state, you are simultaneously intervening on the atoms. Therefore, Hoel’s "macro-interventions" are just micro-interventions wearing a disguise.

**Dewhurst's Conclusion:** Hoel's math doesn't give us new *objects*; it just gives us a formal way to measure Dennett's "Real Patterns." And to Dewhurst, Real Patterns are just highly informative maps, not autonomous territories. 

### 3. How Your RPNC Paper Obliterates Dewhurst’s Concern
Dewhurst’s paper is the exact manifestation of the "Instrumentalist Drift" you identify in Section 1 of RPNC. Dewhurst thinks compression/EI just gives us "better explanations." Your paper argues it gives us **Macro-Objecthood**. 

Here is how RPNC directly answers Dewhurst's roadblocks:

**A. Defeating the "Fat-Handed" Objection via Admissibility**
Dewhurst argues that macro-interventions are "fat-handed" because they always entail a micro-intervention. 
*   **Your Fix (RPNC §2.4):** You solve this by defining **Admissibility**. You argue that interventions are not just laboratory surgeries performed by humans; they are "naturally occurring physical boundary conditions and energy scales." A cell membrane screens out molecular fluctuations objectively. Because nature intervenes on itself at macro-scales, "fat-handedness" is an artifact of treating interventions as human choices rather than physical ecological constraints.

**B. Defeating Kim's Causal Exclusion via Degeneracy**
Dewhurst bows to Kim's argument, assuming micro-sufficiency means macro-redundancy.
*   **Your Fix (RPNC §5.2):** You tackle Kim head-on. You argue that macro-states are non-redundant because they **filter out causal degeneracy**. Because multiple micro-states lead to the same macro-transition (Strong Lumpability), tracking the micro-state actively introduces "non-difference-making noise." The macro-variable is the true difference-maker. 

**C. Upgrading Real Patterns via Closure**
Dewhurst uses Real Patterns to *downgrade* Hoel (saying Hoel hasn't found real objects, just patterns). 
*   **Your Fix:** You use Rosas's math (Computational Closure / Strong Lumpability) to *upgrade* Real Patterns. You argue that if a pattern achieves Transition Autonomy (Closure), it graduates from a "mere description" into a robust Macro-Object.

### 2. Suggestions for the R&R: Expanding on Noise, Energy, and Substrate Independence

Your intuitions here are spot on. You are moving from a purely logical argument to a **thermodynamic/physical** one. If a reviewer asks, *"Why do these macro-objects actually form in nature?"* or *"How does this explain abstract objects?"*, you can use the following concepts. 

Here is how to phrase these ideas so they sound like rigorous philosophy of science rather than speculative metaphysics.

#### A. Standing Up to Micro-Level Noise (Filtering Causal Degeneracy)
You need to explain *why* ignoring the micro-level is actually an advantage, not just a shortcut.
*   **The Argument:** At the micro-level, physical systems are incredibly noisy (Brownian motion, thermal fluctuations, quantum uncertainty). If a macro-object’s future depended on every single micro-fluctuation, it would dissolve instantly. Macro-patterns matter because they **filter out causal degeneracy**. Because multiple micro-states map to the exact same macro-transition (Strong Lumpability), the macro-boundary acts as a physical shield. It screens off the non-difference-making noise. 
*   **How to phrase it defensibly:** *"A closed macro-partition is not merely an epistemic convenience; it is an error-correction mechanism. By screening off micro-level noise and causal degeneracy, the macro-state isolates the actual difference-making variables required for systemic transitions."*

#### B. The Energetic / Computational Cost of Survival
This is where you bring in the structure of the universe (thermodynamics) to explain *why* these objects possess staying power.
*   **The Argument:** Information processing has a physical, thermodynamic cost (Landauer’s Principle). For a physical system (like a cell or an organism) to maintain itself, it must predict its environment. If it tries to track every atom, it will suffer a computational explosion and exhaust its energy. Reality selects for macro-objects because they are **thermodynamically efficient**. 
*   **How to phrase it defensibly:** *"The emergence of closure is driven by physical constraints. Tracking full micro-detail incurs unsustainable computational and energetic overhead. A partition that successfully achieves macro-closure dimensionally reduces the state space without losing predictive power, granting the system structural staying power in a thermodynamically constrained universe."*

#### C. Substrate Independence and Projectibility
You want to explain how completely closed macro-objects become substrate-independent, which explains why they are projectible.
*   **The Argument:** If a macro-object is *perfectly* closed, its future transitions rely entirely on its current macro-state. It doesn't need to "look down" at its atoms to know what to do next. Because it doesn't rely on its atoms, it can be transferred to *different* atoms without breaking. This is why we can "project" the behavior of a hurricane or a neural network across different environments.
*   **How to phrase it defensibly:** *"As a partition approaches strict computational closure, its reliance on specific micro-implementations approaches zero. This provides a naturalistic grounding for multiple realizability and projectibility: a pattern is projectible across diverse contexts precisely because its transition autonomy is substrate-independent."*

#### D. The Limit Case: Abstract Objects
You want to explain abstract objects (like math or chess) without sounding like a mystic. You can do this by treating them as the extreme end of the closure spectrum.
*   **The Argument:** A hurricane is *mostly* closed, but still requires physics. The rules of chess or the number 7 are *perfectly* closed. Their transition rules (e.g., $2+2=4$) are completely self-contained. They are abstract not because they exist in a magical Platonic heaven, but because they have **zero substrate leakage**. 
*   **How to phrase it defensibly:** *"This dynamical framework also offers a deflationary account of formal or abstract objects. Rather than positing a separate Platonic ontology, formal systems (such as arithmetic or chess) can be understood as the theoretical limit case of closure: patterns that achieve perfect transition autonomy with zero substrate leakage. Their 'abstractness' is simply a measure of their absolute multiple realizability."*

### How to Use This in the Peer Review Process
If a reviewer tells you that your account is "too pragmatic" or asks you to explain the physical grounding of your theory, you can deploy **A and B** to anchor your theory in thermodynamics. 

If a reviewer asks, *"But what about mathematics and abstract models?"* (which they almost certainly will), you can deploy **C and D** to show that your framework is mathematically elegant enough to include abstract objects as "limit cases" on a continuous spectrum of closure.
