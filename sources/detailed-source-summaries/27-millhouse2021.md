# millhouse2021

## Full Citation
Tyler Millhouse, "Really Real Patterns," *Australasian Journal of Philosophy* 100, no. 4 (2022): 664-678. https://doi.org/10.1080/00048402.2021.1941153.

## Detailed Summary
Millhouse argues that Dennett's real-patterns framework captures an important connection between compression and objective regularity, but remains too permissive for robust realism. The core concern is that predictive economy alone cannot discriminate between models that predict equally well while proposing different internal structures. On Millhouse's diagnosis, this leaves real patterns vulnerable to instrumentalist readings.

The proposed fix is to shift from compression-and-regularity to compression-and-similarity. Millhouse develops a criterion of pattern instantiation using ideas from algorithmic information theory. A pattern is well-instantiated when there is a relatively simple mapping from the physical system's states to the model's states that also preserves the model's transition structure under counterfactual variation. Simplicity is cashed out in Kolmogorov-complexity terms: shorter mapping programs indicate tighter structural match.

This criterion is presented in three parts for dynamical models: a mapping constraint (there must be a state-space mapping), a counterfactual constraint (mapped trajectories must respect model dynamics), and a simplicity criterion (among eligible mappings, simpler ones support stronger instantiation claims). The argument is that this framework can distinguish genuine structure-tracking models from gerrymandered interpretations that only mimic outputs.

Millhouse uses Block's blockhead as an instructive case. The blockhead can be behaviorally interpretable, but the mapping from its internal states to intentional states would require importing substantial external interpretive machinery. That raises mapping complexity and weakens the claim that intentional structure is genuinely instantiated by the system itself. By contrast, systems with internal organization that directly supports the modeled states admit simpler mappings.

A further contribution is methodological flexibility. Millhouse allows that instantiation can be assessed at different abstraction levels, but insists we must be explicit about which ontology-level is being tested. High-level success can vindicate high-level postulates without automatically vindicating lower-level implementation details.

## Core Argument Map
- **Problem:** Dennett-style compression criteria risk underdetermining ontology when rival models are similarly predictive.
- **Key move:** Replace a pure regularity test with a similarity-sensitive instantiation test.
- **Formal proposal:** Require mapping, counterfactual preservation, and mapping simplicity.
- **Anti-gerrymandering function:** Complex ad hoc mappings count against realist instantiation claims.
- **Case support:** Blockhead looks behaviorally adequate but structurally poor under the simplicity test.
- **Conclusion:** Real patterns can be made more realist by tying commitment to structural fit, not predictive economy alone.

## Relation to Real Patterns Need Closure
This paper is directly relevant to RPNC's anti-gerrymandering and realism-discipline goals. Millhouse sharpens the same pressure point that RPNC targets: predictive success by itself is too weak for ontological commitment. His mapping-simplicity framework is not identical to closure, but it is complementary. Both approaches try to prevent permissive model selection from inflating ontology.

For RPNC, Millhouse is useful as a bridge citation: he motivates why stronger admissibility and structure-preservation constraints are needed beyond compression. Closure can then be presented as a dynamical strengthening in the transition domain, where commitment is indexed to whether macro-transition structure is preserved under declared constraints.

## Reviewer-Relevant Cautions
- Millhouse's criterion depends on an algorithmic-complexity framing that is conceptually strong but often difficult to operationalize directly in empirical practice.
- The proposal leaves open weighting questions among mapping simplicity, model simplicity, and predictive reliability.
- The account is compatible with multi-level assessment; high-level vindication does not automatically settle low-level realization disputes.
- Use in RPNC should avoid overstating Millhouse as already providing closure; his contribution is an instantiation discipline, not a lumpability-style transition criterion.
