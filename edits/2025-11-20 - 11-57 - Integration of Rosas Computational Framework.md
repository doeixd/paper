## Overview

This edit integrates "Software in the natural world: A computational approach to emergence in complex multi-level systems" (Rosas et al., 2024) into the information-to-consciousness appendix. The integration provides rigorous computational/causal grounding that complements the existing quantum/epistemic foundations from Ayvazov.

## Rationale

While Ayvazov (2025) provided the quantum/epistemic justification for the framework's information-theoretic foundations, Rosas et al. (2024) provides the computational/causal justification. Together, these papers form two complementary validation layers:

- **Ayvazov:** Why does information structure exist? (Quantum/epistemic layer)
- **Rosas et al.:** How does structure persist and act? (Computational/causal layer)
- **This paper:** What does it feel like and how do we use it? (Phenomenological/social layer)

## Key Theoretical Contributions from Rosas et al.

1. **ε-machines vs υ-machines:** The ε-machine (epsilon-machine) constructs predictions using only macro-level data; the υ-machine (upsilon-machine) uses micro-level data to predict macro-level behavior. When these are equivalent, computational closure is achieved.

2. **Strong vs Weak Lumpability:** Weak lumpability works for specific initial conditions; strong lumpability works regardless of substrate details, representing genuine substrate independence.

3. **Causal Decoupling:** When computational closure succeeds, the macro-level becomes causally autonomous—it "runs software" independent of "hardware."

4. **Lattice Theory:** All valid coarse-grainings form a mathematical lattice; the Apex Network represents the optimal path through this lattice.

5. **Control Theory:** Causally closed systems can be efficiently controlled through macro-level interventions (engaging the ε-machine) rather than substrate manipulation.
