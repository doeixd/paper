## Changes Made

### Addition 1: Section 3.2.2 "Model Complexity as Compression Failure"

**Location**: After Section 3.2.1, before Section 3.3
**Length**: ~400 words (3 paragraphs)

**Purpose**: Provide deeper conceptual grounding for why Model Complexity $M(t)$ functions as a brittleness indicator.

**Core Argument**:
- Viable theories function as compression algorithms, reducing vast empirical data into compact generative principles
- Deep compression (e.g., Newton's laws) enables genuine prediction beyond training data
- The "incompressibility trap": when theory complexity approaches data complexity, the theory becomes a lookup table with minimal predictive power
- Rising $M(t)$ signals loss of compression ratio, indicating structural brittleness
- Reframes Occam's Razor as an epistemic necessity, not aesthetic preference

**Key Features**:
- No formal equations; purely qualitative explanation
- Uses Newton vs. Ptolemy as conceptual illustration
- Maintains measured, analytical tone

### Addition 2: Section 4.7 "The Dynamics of Paradigm Transition"

**Location**: After Section 4.6, before Section 5
**Length**: ~700 words (9 paragraphs)

**Purpose**: Explain why high-brittleness systems persist for centuries and what triggers paradigm shifts (punctuated equilibrium pattern).

**Core Argument**:
- Knowledge systems are material/institutional investments, not just abstract propositions
- Two cost streams determine stability:
  1. **Cost of maintenance**: Daily, accumulating costs of patches, coercion, complexity management
  2. **Cost of transition**: Catastrophic one-time reorganization cost
- Systems remain stable while perceived transition cost > maintenance burden
- **Tipping point**: When accumulated maintenance exceeds transition cost, system becomes vulnerable to paradigm shift
- Explains punctuated equilibrium: long stability → sudden rupture (not smooth slides)
- External shocks are clarificatory (spike maintenance costs) not causal
- "Early adopters" recognize crossing point before consensus, bear social costs as nucleation points
- Complements Section 4.6 by explaining what unlocks fitness traps

**Key Features**:
- No formal equations or pseudo-quantification
- Avoids introducing new technical terminology (uses descriptive phrases)
- Explicitly connects to existing Section 4.6 content
- Includes appropriate caveats ("not a deterministic prediction")
- Uses Ptolemaic-to-Copernican transition as concrete illustration
- Maintains measured academic tone
