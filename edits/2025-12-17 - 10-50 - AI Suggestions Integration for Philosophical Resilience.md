## Changes Made

### 1. Real Patterns Definition (Section 2.1, ~line 39)
**Before:** Brief reference to Dennett and Ladyman & Ross without elaboration.

**After:** Added explicit distinction between *material substrate* and *causal structure*. Real Patterns now defined by what they do to probability distributions (supporting counterfactual interventions), not merely by material composition.

**Rationale:** Strengthens the information-theoretic ontology by making clear that existence is about causal efficacy, not material constitution.

---

### 2. Mathematical Inevitability of Markov Blankets (Section 3.1, ~line 230)
**Addition:** New paragraph explaining that boundary formation follows from basic physical constraints (causality, locality, information). Markov blankets emerge naturally from local interaction constraints, not observer choice.

**Rationale:** Moves Markov blankets from "biological observation" to "logical inevitability," making it harder for critics to dismiss them as useful fictions.

---

### 3. Traffic Jam Analogy (Section 3.4, ~line 337)
**Addition:** New paragraph with traffic jam example complementing the Chess analogy. Illustrates ε-machine/υ-machine distinction for continuous dynamical systems.

**Rationale:** Chess is discrete and rule-bound; traffic density better illustrates thermodynamic and biological contexts where the paper claims broadest applicability.

---

### 4. Triangle Representation Puzzle (Section 4A.3, ~line 614)
**Addition:** New paragraph addressing "where is the triangle?" category error. The triangle exists at the ε-machine level as a macro-state achieving computational closure.

**Rationale:** Bridges the gap between abstract "structural constraints" language and the reader's actual experience of mental imagery, making the consciousness account more intuitive.

---

### 5. Mereological Nihilism Rebuttal (Section 6.1, ~line 818)
**Addition:** New paragraph directly confronting the "fundamental particles arranged structure-wise" objection. Arrangements create new information-flow constraints; denying emergent wholes would imply that causal efficacy is insufficient for existence—a standard that would eliminate even fundamental particles.

**Rationale:** The paper assumed readers accept emergence if the math works. This addition provides the philosophical defense against reductionist objections that arrangements are ontologically neutral.
