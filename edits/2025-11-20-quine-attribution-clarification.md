## Problem Identified

User raised critical concern: "My main concern is misusing quine / misunderstanding / conflicting and not saying so" and "not giving correct credit / relating our things to him, and saying why we are naming."

Initial audit revealed:
1. We correctly cited Quine's core concepts (web of belief, dispositions to assent, holism, center-periphery)
2. BUT we added substantial structure (5 tiers, Standing Predicates, 5-stage transformation, 3 levels of truth) without always explicitly distinguishing "Quine's ideas" from "our elaborations"
3. Risk of appearing to attribute our systematization to Quine
4. Potential violation of Quinean holism's spirit by introducing sharp tier boundaries

---

## Solution Implemented

### Primary Fix: New Section 1.6 (Lines 85-123)

Added comprehensive section titled "Relation to Quine's Framework" with four subsections:

**"What We Take from Quine"**
- Explicitly lists six core Quinean commitments with citations:
  1. Thoroughgoing naturalism (dispositions to assent)
  2. Holism (no isolated testing)
  3. Center-periphery structure
  4. Pragmatic revision
  5. Revisability in principle
  6. Recalcitrant experience
- Cites specific works: Word and Object (1960), Two Dogmas (1951), Web of Belief (1978)

**"What We Add Beyond Quine"**
- Explicitly identifies six extensions with clear attribution:
  1. Five-tier architecture (analytical tool, not ontological)
  2. "Standing Predicates" terminology (our term for "well-entrenched beliefs")
  3. Five-stage functional transformation mechanism (making Quine's insights testable)
  4. Three levels of truth (preserving deflation, adding structure)
  5. Differential activation dynamics (extending Quine's cost-benefit analysis)
  6. Information-theoretic formalism (Pearl, Friston - modern, not Quine)
- For each, explains it's OUR addition, not Quine's

**"Why These Additions"**
- Justifies extensions as serving Quine's naturalistic project:
  1. Make web metaphor empirically testable
  2. Resolve problems Quine left open (isolation objection)
  3. Systematize scattered Quinean insights
- Shows we're developing Quine, not betraying him

**"One Tension: System vs. Holism"**
- Honestly acknowledges potential conflict: five tiers might violate Quine's resistance to sharp boundaries
- Defends systematization as analytical rather than ontological
- Emphasizes boundaries are porous, predicates on continua
- Positions as making Quine explicit, not replacing him

**"Continuity with Quine's Project"**
- Affirms fidelity to Quine's naturalistic vision despite extensions
- Shows philosophical continuity: "Where Quine provided metaphor and foundations, we supply mechanical details"
