## Changes Made

### 1. Expanded ยง3.4 into "What the Formal Machinery Alone Does Not Provide"

Replaced the 3-paragraph causal-state equivalence discussion with a structured novelty argument naming four things the formal literature alone does not supply:

1. **Intervention indexing**: Causal-state constructions are observational-predictive. This criterion adds admissible interventions as a parameter.
2. **Explicit ontological interpretation**: Lumpability tells you when transitions are preserved. It does not tell you when to make realist commitments. That's philosophical work.
3. **Graded commitment protocol**: The formal literature offers exact conditions and approximate measures, not a framework for translating measures into warranted verdicts with robustness requirements and downgrade rules.
4. **Anti-gerrymandering argument**: The Dennett permissiveness problem is a philosophical problem. Lumpability provides a tool; the argument that closure is the right anti-gerrymandering condition is a philosophical claim, not a theorem.

Also added a clean causal-emergence contrast: EI gains are a valuable diagnostic but don't settle which macro-descriptions deserve realist commitment, since a partition can increase effective information while remaining fragile under intervention or horizon variation.

A criterion that granted robust objecthood regardless of leakiness would be the permissive framework the paper replaces. The gain is matching evidential reality rather than forcing binary realism/eliminativism.
