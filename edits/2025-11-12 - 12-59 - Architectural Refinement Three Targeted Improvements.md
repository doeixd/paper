## Overview

## Change 1: Streamlined Section 2.1 (Major Structural Revision)

### Location
Section 2.1, "From Individual Dispositions to Functional Propositions: A Naturalistic Progression"

### What Changed
Replaced the extensive six-subsection treatment (2.1.1 through 2.1.6) with a more concise four-subsection version:
- **Old structure:** 2.1.1 (Belief as Disposition), 2.1.2 (Conscious Awareness), 2.1.3 (Social Coordination), 2.1.4 (Functional Propositions), 2.1.5 (Complete Progression), 2.1.6 (Indeterminacy)
- **New structure:** 2.1.1 (Quinean Foundation), 2.1.2 (Phenomenological Bridge), 2.1.3 (From Awareness to Public Claim), 2.1.4 (Social Stabilization)

### Rationale
The original Section 2.1 was philosophically thorough but strategically problematic:

1. **Excessive Attack Surface:** The detailed discussion of conscious awareness, phenomenological experience, and the six-stage progression from sensation to Standing Predicate invited complex objections from philosophy of mind and language that could distract from the paper's core contribution (the brittleness framework and Apex Network theory).

2. **Conceptual Bloat:** Introduced more terminology and conceptual machinery than strictly necessary to ground the framework. Parsimony strengthens philosophical arguments by reducing the number of commitments requiring defense.

3. **Pacing Issues:** Forced readers to digest an almost freestanding philosophical argument about belief and propositions before reaching the novel theoretical contributions. The most effective strategy is to establish foundations quickly and cleanly, then develop the distinctive ideas.

### What Was Preserved
Despite the substantial condensation, the new version maintains all essential philosophical commitments:
- Quinean behaviorism as the foundation (dispositions to assent)
- The role of conscious awareness as the bridge to public knowledge
- The deflationary account of propositions as coordination devices
- The treatment of Standing Predicates as evolved epistemic tools
- The response to Quine's indeterminacy thesis (now integrated into 2.1.4)
- The normativity claim (epistemic norms as hypothetical imperatives)

### What Was Streamlined
- Removed the extended "Sarah and the bus" example (illustrative but not essential)
- Condensed the six-stage progression into a more direct narrative flow
- Integrated the indeterminacy discussion into the main text rather than treating it as a separate subsection
- Eliminated some redundant explanatory passages

### Philosophical Benefit
The revision makes the foundational argument more resilient by:
- Reducing vulnerability to tangential criticisms
- Maintaining philosophical precision with fewer moving parts
- Allowing readers to reach the novel theoretical contributions (brittleness diagnostics, Apex Network emergence) more quickly
- Demonstrating that the framework's core insights do not depend on any particular detailed theory of mental content

## Change 2: Refined Section 1.2 Key Terminology (Minor but Strategic)

### Location
Section 1.2, "Key Terminology"

### What Changed
Simplified the introductory terminology section to focus only on the paper's novel concepts, removing premature definitions of Quinean foundational terms.

**Old version:**
> "This paper develops several core concepts. Systemic brittleness refers to the accumulated costs that signal a knowledge system's vulnerability. A disposition to assent is a behavioral readiness to affirm a sentence. The conscious awareness of such a disposition is what we functionally identify as a belief. The articulated sentence that a disposition is about serves as a functional proposition. When such a claim is socially validated, it may become a Standing Predicate: a reusable conceptual tool. The Apex Network is the emergent, objective structure of maximally viable dispositional patterns. For a complete glossary, see Appendix B."

**New version:**
> "This paper develops several core concepts to execute its argument. Systemic brittleness refers to the accumulated costs that signal a knowledge system's vulnerability to failure. We argue that through a process of pragmatic selection, validated claims can become Standing Predicates: reusable, action-guiding conceptual tools. This evolutionary process drives knowledge systems toward the Apex Network, which we define as the emergent, objective structure of maximally viable solutions determined by mind-independent constraints. A complete glossary is available in Appendix B."

### Rationale
Defining technical terms before the philosophical work justifying them creates a logical inversion. The introduction should preview the paper's novel contributions, not frontload Quinean terminology that Section 2.1 will properly develop. This creates a cleaner logical flow:
1. Introduction previews novel concepts (brittleness, Standing Predicates, Apex Network)
2. Section 2.1 builds the Quinean foundation
3. Section 2.2+ develops the framework using these foundations

### Philosophical Benefit
Eliminates the awkwardness of defining "disposition to assent," "belief," and "functional proposition" before explaining their theoretical role. The revised version is more pedagogically sound and reduces the impression of introducing unnecessary jargon.

## Change 3: Enhanced Section 4.6 Power Discussion (Targeted Integration)

### Location
Section 4.6, "Navigating the Landscape: Fitness Traps, Path Dependence, and the Role of Power," fourth paragraph

### What Changed
Revised the explanation of power's constitutive role to be more precise and better integrated with the framework's diagnostic tools.

**Old version (excerpt):**
> "...institutional power actively digs the fitness trap and locks the system into a high-brittleness state."

**New version (excerpt):**
> "...institutional power can lock a system into a high-brittleness state. This is not an unmeasurable phenomenon; the costs of this constitutive power manifest as diagnosable symptoms, such as suppressed innovation (a low I(t) ratio) and the need for escalating ideological enforcement, which registers as rising Coercive Overheads (C(t))."

### Rationale
Two improvements:
1. **Precision:** Replaced the informal metaphor "digs the fitness trap" with the more precise "construct and enforce the very parameters of a fitness trap."
2. **Integration:** Explicitly linked the abstract discussion of power back to the paper's diagnostic indicators (I(t) innovation lag and C(t) coercive overheads), demonstrating that the framework provides concrete tools for detecting power-masked brittleness.

### Philosophical Benefit
Shows that the framework is not merely descriptive but offers practical diagnostic capabilities. The power dynamics discussion is now tightly integrated with the brittleness measurement apparatus, making the theoretical and practical components work together seamlessly.
