## Change 5: Sharpening the Brittleness-Truth Distinction (Section 4.3)

### Location
Section 4.3, "A Three-Level Framework for Truth," added sentence after introductory paragraph

### What Changed

**Original ending:**
> "It reframes truth as a status propositions earn through increasingly rigorous stages of validation."

**New ending (added sentence):**
> "It reframes truth as a status propositions earn through increasingly rigorous stages of validation. Crucially, in this model, a demonstrated track record of low systemic brittleness functions as our primary fallible evidence for a system's alignment with the Apex Network; it is not, itself, constitutive of that alignment."

### Rationale

**Critical distinction:** Explicitly separates:
- **Epistemic category:** Low brittleness (our evidence for truth)
- **Metaphysical category:** Alignment with Apex Network (what truth is)

**Prevents misreading:** Without this clarification, a skeptical reviewer could charge that the framework conflates justification (low brittleness) with truth itself. This sentence makes the relationship unambiguous.

**Reinforces fallibilism:** By explicitly stating brittleness is "fallible evidence," the framework maintains its commitment to epistemic humility while grounding realism.

### Impact
Closes the final gap a critic could exploit to mischaracterize the framework as pragmatist anti-realism. The distinction between evidence-for and constitution-of truth is now explicit and unimpeachable.
