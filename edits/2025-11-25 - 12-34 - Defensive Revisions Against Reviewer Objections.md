## Revision 4: Agency Terminology Clarification (Section 4.5, lines 620 and 645)

**Vulnerability:** The phrase "self-caused variation" is conceptually problematic in a deterministic framework. Critics could object: "How can anything be 'self-caused' if causation is closed?"

**Solution:** Replaced "self-caused variation" with "internally-generated variation" throughout:

**Changes made:**
- Line 620: "Agency is not freedom from causation but internally-generated variation within constraint space."
- Added clarification: "The variation originates from the system's own dynamics rather than external perturbation, though it remains causally constrained by the system's structure and history."
- Line 645: Changed "self-generated variation" to "internally-generated variation" for consistency

**Conceptual improvement:**
- "Internally-generated" is clearer and less philosophically loaded than "self-caused"
- The added sentence explains the distinction: variation source (internal vs external) rather than causal status (caused vs uncaused)
- Maintains compatibilist framework while removing apparent contradiction
- Preserves intuition that agency comes from within the system without claiming causal independence

**Rationale:** This is a precise terminological fix that closes a conceptual vulnerability. "Self-caused" invites objections about causal closure; "internally-generated" accurately captures what the framework claims (variation originates in system's own dynamics) without the problematic "self-caused" language.
