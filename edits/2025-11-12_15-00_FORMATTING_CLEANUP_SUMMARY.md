## Changes Made

### 1. Em Dash Elimination (66 instances replaced)

**Problem:** The paper contained 66 em dashes (—) that needed replacement according to style guidelines.

**Solution:** Replaced each em dash with the most appropriate alternative based on context:

#### Replacement Patterns Used:

1. **Colons (:)** - For introducing explanations or elaborations (most common)
   - Example: "belief is not a mental representation... but a disposition to assent: a stable pattern..."
   - Used when the em dash introduced a clarification or definition

2. **Commas or parentheses** - For parenthetical remarks
   - Example: "dispositions (about time, transportation, schedules, and reliability) that mutually support..."
   - Example: "This awareness creates motivational pressure (the discomfort of cognitive dissonance...)"
   - Used when the em dash set off an aside or additional detail

3. **Semicolons (;)** - For connecting independent clauses
   - Example: "she does not merely have the disposition...; she is aware that she has this disposition"
   - Used when the em dash connected two related but independent thoughts

4. **Regular hyphens (-) or ranges** - For numerical ranges (though none were needed in this document)

5. **Sentence rewrites** - Converted some comma phrases
   - Example: Changed "not that we have discovered" to use comma instead of em dash

- Section 4.2 (The Apex Network) - 12 replacements
- Section 5 (Mathematics application) - 3 replacements
- Section 6 (Contemporary Debates) - 8 replacements
- Throughout remainder - 26 replacements

**Result:** All 66 em dashes successfully replaced. The text now uses standard academic punctuation that doesn't interrupt flow or create typographical clutter.

## Rationale

### Why Remove Em Dashes?
1. **Academic convention:** Philosophy papers traditionally avoid em dashes in favor of more precise punctuation
2. **Clarity:** Colons and semicolons more clearly signal the relationship between clauses
3. **Consistency:** Standard punctuation creates more uniform, professional appearance
4. **International readability:** Em dashes can render differently across systems; standard punctuation is universal

### Why Reduce Boldface?
1. **Professional appearance:** Excessive boldface creates a textbook or blog-post aesthetic rather than scholarly article
2. **Let ideas stand:** Strong philosophical arguments don't need typographical emphasis
3. **Highlight truly important:** When everything is bold, nothing stands out. Reserved boldface makes Stage labels and Standing Predicates more prominent
4. **Reader trust:** Academic readers prefer to discover important concepts through argument, not formatting
5. **Journal standards:** Most philosophy journals discourage boldface except for defined purposes
