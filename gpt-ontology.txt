# **Realism through Modal Constraint Convergence:

## **Abstract**

*(Already written above; included for completeness.)*

This paper develops a unified epistemology and ontology that explains how objective structure emerges from the distributed, fallible practices of embodied agents. At the epistemic level, I advance **Emergent Pragmatic Coherentism**, the view that belief networks evolve toward greater stability not through correspondence to pre-given facts, but through iterative interactions with the pragmatic constraints of the world. On this account, cognitive systems, cultural lineages, and scientific communities are coherence-driven networks continuously filtered by the costs of misprediction, failed coping, and maladaptive behavior. Objectivity arises when these independently constructed networks converge under shared pressures, yielding stable, cross-agent patterns of inference.

This epistemology is paired with a complementary metaphysical thesis I call **Realism through Modal Constraint Convergence**. Rather than positing a fixed ontology waiting to be discovered, this view holds that the real is the set of structural attractors that persist across variations in perspective, culture, and conceptual scheme. These attractors emerge because the modal structure of the world—its constraints on perception, action, evolution, and learning—filters out unstable alternatives over time. What becomes “objectively real” is the residue that survives this long-run filtering process: the convergent features of an environment that systematically discipline biological, cognitive, and cultural systems.

Together, these two theses offer a naturalistic account of objectivity without foundationalism or metaphysical absolutism. The framework shows how epistemic convergence can be explained by deep modal constraints rather than subjective consensus or Platonic correspondence. A case study on cross-cultural color preference illustrates how seemingly subjective domains exhibit stable, species-wide attractors shaped by evolutionary and environmental pressures. The resulting picture is one in which objectivity is neither arbitrary nor transcendent, but an emergent product of constraint-guided convergence in a world that pushes back.

# **2. Epistemology: Emergent Pragmatic Coherentism**

## **2.1 From foundationalism to coherence to pragmatism**

Traditional epistemology has oscillated between:

* **Foundationalism** (knowledge rests on secure, privileged givens), and
* **Coherentism** (beliefs justify one another in a network).

Foundationalism offers firmness but cannot explain why the “givens” should be trusted. Coherentism avoids dogmatism but struggles to explain how coherence relates to truth rather than collectively reinforced delusion.

**Pragmatic naturalism** offers a third path: inferential networks survive by *working*—by sustaining successful prediction, coordination, manipulation, or coping. But classical pragmatism often fails to explain how shared structures arise across diverse agents.

Emergent Pragmatic Coherentism brings these pieces together.

## **2.2 Cognitive systems as coherence networks**

On this view:

* Each agent maintains a **Shared Network**, a Quinean web of belief, expectation, and habit.
* Local coherence determines internal viability.
* Pragmatic success determines external viability.

Agents revise beliefs not merely to reduce contradiction, but to avoid pragmatic penalties—failed predictions, wasted energy, social costs, physical danger.

Thus, epistemic revision is:

> **Coherence constrained by reality’s pushback.**

A diagram here would show a belief-network gradually reshaped by “error pressure” from environmental constraints.

## **2.3 Why convergence occurs**

If all agents face the same macro-level constraints—gravity, energy availability, resource foraging challenges, chemical regularities—then despite differing starting points, selection pressures favor similar structural solutions.

This produces:

* cross-agent convergence,
* culture-to-culture convergence,
* lineage-to-lineage convergence.

The result is a kind of **distributed Bayesian updating**, but realized through evolutionary, cultural, and cognitive dynamics.

## **2.4 Objectivity as pragmatic stability across networks**

Objectivity arises when:

* different networks

> **Realism through Modal Constraint Convergence** —

Quinean webs explain coherence but not convergence. They cannot explain why independently developed webs arrive at similar structures. The modal constraint layer is what adds:

The framework extends holism into a naturalized theory of objective emergence.

## **5.3 Objection: “This is just structural realism.”**

**Reply:**
It is a descendant of structural realism, but differs in that:

* structures are emergent attractors, not metaphysical essences
* modal constraints replace mind-independent fixed properties
* objectivity is the residue of filtering, not the discovery of pre-given structures

It is a **dynamic, pragmatic structural realism**, not a static or Platonic one.

## **5.4 Objection: “But different cultures can disagree.”**

**Reply:**
Local variation is expected and predicted. The theory posits a **Pluralist Frontier**:
regions of conceptual space where constraints are too weak or too multidimensional to force convergence.

But convergence occurs in:

* geometry
* number systems
* kinship categories
* basic color distinctions
* physical regularities
* causal intuitions

The model predicts both universals and variation.

## **5.5 Objection: “Is this deterministic?”**

**Reply:**
No. Modal constraints create attractors but do not fix exact trajectories. They:

* shape probability distributions
* bias evolutionary paths
* filter cognitive networks

The view is probabilistic and dynamical, not metaphysically determinist.
