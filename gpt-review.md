# Review of *Computational Closure and the Architecture of Mind*

## Abstract

- **Clarity of Claims:** Ensure each claim is clearly framed as a
  proposal, not an established fact. For example, the Abstract's
  statement that the Free Energy Principle (FEP) is *"the necessary
  'grammar of existence' for bounded entities"* is very strong. It may
  be better to soften this by saying the paper *"treats the FEP as a
  constitutive principle of bounded systems"* or *"argues for the FEP as
  a conceptual grounding"* rather than an empirical discovery. Clarify
  that this is a theoretical stance, in line with criticisms that FEP is
  contested (Mangalam 2025).
- **Terminology:** Make sure all key terms (e.g. "hierarchical
  compression," "Standing Predicates," "Apex Network") are succinctly
  defined or previewed. The phrase *"Apex Network---the thermodynamic
  attractor in the space of possible compressions"* is evocative but
  could be confusing without context. Perhaps briefly note that the Apex
  Network will be defined as the set of optimal coarse-grainings
  (compression structures) below.
- **Avoid Overstatement:** Phrases like *"operationalize 'truth' not as
  correspondence"* should be introduced carefully. The idea that truth
  aligns with the Apex Network is a novel redefinition; it would be
  helpful to acknowledge upfront that this is a proposed framework, not
  the only view.
- **Tone and Scope:** The Abstract opens by identifying an "explanatory
  gap" between neural mechanics and normative epistemology. It correctly
  states the paper is *"a work of synthesis, not empirical
  discovery"*[\[1\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=This%20paper%20is%20a%20work,discover%2C%20integrate%20rather%20than%20originate).
  Maintain that humility by ensuring later sentences avoid sounding like
  manifestos. For example, temper language such as *"minimize prediction
  error is the engine that shapes both cognitive architecture and
  epistemic norms"* so it reads as a *theoretical claim to be evaluated*
  rather than an absolute fact.

## 1. Introduction {#introduction}

  between predictive processing and epistemology. Check that each claim
  here is well-qualified. For instance, the suggestion that the drive to
  minimize prediction error *"shapes... epistemic norms"* should be
  presented as a hypothesis. Perhaps cite Friston's account that brains
  minimize free
  energy[\[2\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Contemporary%20theories%20of%20mind%20and,Meanwhile%2C%20naturalized%20epistemology)
  to ground the claim, but note that applying this to epistemic
  justification is speculative.

  "computational closure" and "Markov blankets" immediately. It might
  help to add a one-sentence intuitive gloss for readers unfamiliar with
  these concepts. For example: *"A Markov blanket is a statistical

  watch for overly dense sentences. Break long sentences into shorter
  ones for readability. For example, the sentence at the end of §1 could
  be split:

## 2. Information as Fundamental Substrate {#information-as-fundamental-substrate}

- **Defining Information:** The distinction between Shannon information
  and thermodynamic energy is clear, but ensure terms are defined before
  use. For instance, explain Shannon entropy as average surprise (which
  the text does) and briefly note Landauer's
  Principle[\[4\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=A%20Note%20on%20Entropy%20and,in%20kinetic%20energy%20wasted%20on).
  Possibly add a citation to Shannon (1948) for context.
- **Entropy vs. Energy:** The note on entropy and energy is important.
  Make it more reader-friendly: e.g. *"Erasing one bit of information
  dissipates at least kT ln 2 joules (Landauer 1961). Thus, high
  prediction error (information leakage) has a real energy
  cost[\[4\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=A%20Note%20on%20Entropy%20and,in%20kinetic%20energy%20wasted%20on).
  In cognitive terms, 'epistemic brittleness' (persistent surprise)
  carries thermodynamic penalties."* Define "epistemic brittleness" here
  or shortly after if introduced.
- **"Thing" Existence:** The claim *"A 'thing' exists to the extent it
  maintains statistical boundaries"* is conceptually provocative. It
  might need qualification: note that we are adopting a functionalist
  perspective where existence is relative to successful Markov blankets.
  Possibly cite Friston (2010) or related sources that describe any
  self-organizing system as having a Markov blanket.
- **Free Energy Principle (FEP):** The summary (Section 2.2) is accurate
  in spirit, but emphasize that FEP is a mathematical formulation of
  self-organization (Friston 2010). Point out that failing to minimize
  free energy leads to dissipation (system vanishing). But also note
  critiques: e.g. Mangalam (2025) argues FEP is too broad. Clarify this
  paper treats FEP as a meta-theoretical constraint, not a debatable
  empirical hypothesis.
- **Tone:** Avoid phrases like "thermodynamic, not teleological." It's
  fine to say "non-teleological" but perhaps drop "fundamental," since
  readers may over-interpret. Emphasize that we are describing necessary
  conditions for persistence, not goals.
- **Flow:** Make sure transitions between information theory and
  predictive processing are smooth. For example, after the Landauer
  note, you could explicitly connect: *"Hence, information-theoretic
  costs map onto physical costs in any system that learns. The Free
  Energy Principle formalizes this: any self-organizing system minimizes
  surprise by updating its internal model or changing the world, which
  from physics means minimizing dissipation."*

## 3. Markov Blankets: The Architecture of Existence {#markov-blankets-the-architecture-of-existence}

- **3.1 Definition and Example:** The cell example is clear and
  intuitive[\[5\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Consider%20a%20living%20cell,statistical%20boundary%20creating%20conditional%20independence).
  Possibly cite Friston's work on Markov blankets (Friston 2013, etc.)
  to anchor the concept academically. When giving the formal condition
  *P(μ\|s,a,η) = P(μ\|s,a)*, clarify in words: "The internal state μ is
  independent of external causes η once sensory (s) and active (a)
  states are known."
- **3.2 Ontological Implications:** The claim that *"what exists depends
  on the blanket"* is bold. The text does a good job anticipating
  relativist objections. In the plan, note that this needs extra care:
  emphasize that while an observer could *choose* a boundary
  arbitrarily, not every choice yields a self-contained system. As the
  text says, physics "resists" bad
  blankets[\[6\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Reality%20acts%20as%20the%20resistance,of%20the%20blanket%20is%20objective).
  Perhaps add a brief analogy or reference (e.g. Kuhnian paradigms or
  Hobbes' semantic perspective) to contextualize this epistemic
  relativity in philosophy of science.
- **Empirical Examples:** The discussion of cells, phlogiston, quarks is
  effective. Ensure the examples are clearly labeled as *illustrations*,
  not as empirical experiments. For instance, the phlogiston example
  correctly shows a failed concept via information
  leakage[\[7\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=2.%20,is%20why%20phlogiston%20was%20abandoned).
  Possibly cite historical sources (like Kuhn's *Structure of Scientific
  Revolutions*) to align with this perspective.
- **Logical Flow:** The paragraph beginning *"Blanket-relativity is not
  antirealism"*[\[8\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=The%20crucial%20insight%3A%20blanket,reality%20dictates%20which%20maps%20succeed)
  is crucial. It might be tightened: clarify that the constraint
  structure of the world (e.g. oxygen's role in combustion) objectively
  invalidates the phlogiston blanket, making some ontologies unviable.
  This defends a "constraint-driven pluralism."
- **3.3 Computational Closure:** This subsection (lumpability,
  Markovianness, causal shielding) is well
  defined[\[9\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Lumpability%3A%20Can%20we%20group%20micro,internal%20implementation%20from%20external%20observation).
  Suggest adding citations to Rosas et al. (2024) or others for these
  formal definitions of emergence. The summary line *"emergence has
  succeeded: a new causal level exists"* is appropriate but could note
  this is a specific formal criterion of emergence (not the only notion
  of emergence).
- **Examples of Closure:** The temperature example and phlogiston
  reappear effectively in section 6; perhaps just preview here that
  macro-variables (like temperature) predict without micro detail,
  illustrating lumpability. Keep these points distinct and avoid
  repetition between sections 3 and 6.
- **3.4 ε-Machines:** The chess analogy is clear and
  engaging[\[10\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Imagine%20two%20chess%20players%20trying,the%20outcome%20of%20a%20game).
  It exemplifies causal closure: once board position suffices to predict
  next moves, history is irrelevant. That said, ensure the text doesn't
  overstate: specify that this is an analogy for systems where
  macro-states fully capture relevant information. Perhaps briefly note
  that ε-machines are a construct from computational mechanics
  (Crutchfield 1994) and some readers may not know the term.
- **Functional Analogies:** The section on dispositions, Standing
  Predicates, and the Apex Network as ε-machines is intriguing. Make
  sure it's clear that these are proposed analogies, not formal
  identifications. For example, "Standing Predicates are cultural
  ε-machines" is metaphorical. It might be helpful to footnote or
  clarify that this is a conceptual parallel.
- **3.5--3.6 Ontology and Standings:** If the text has a "Hot Dog
  Paradox" (mentioned at \[2\] but not opened), check that it's properly
  explained: it likely shows how different blankets lead to different
  valid categories. Ensure it's clearly a *conceptual illustration*, not
  an argument based on data. Likewise, the process of notion-to-blanket
  formation (Crusoe example of fire) should be phrased as a *thought
  experiment*: emphasize what it illustrates (single-instance structural
  pattern recognition), not empirical generalization.

## 4. From Information Processing to Consciousness {#from-information-processing-to-consciousness}

- **Scope and Limitations:** The distinction between the **Hard
  Problem** and the **Structural Problem** is
  helpful[\[11\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Scope%20of%20Claims%3A%20We%20must,feels%20different%20from%20mere%20familiarity).
  Emphasize that this section intentionally focuses on the latter
  (functional correlates of consciousness) without solving why
  experience exists at all. This humility should remain in revisions
  (the text does well here).
- **4.1 Conscious vs Unconscious:** The core hypothesis that
  consciousness correlates with *structural pattern recognition* is a
  bold claim. Frame it clearly as a hypothesis, not a proven fact. The
  two-column comparison (unconscious = statistical, conscious =
  structural) is effective, but ensure it doesn't come off as
  definitive. Possibly cite supporting views, e.g. Marcus (2001) on
  symbolic reasoning vs association, to back the idea that
  algebraic/structural processing is distinctive (Marcus 2001 could be
  added if available). The given examples (learning to ride a bike,
  recognizing contradictions) are intuitive; just ensure the text says
  these are *illustrations*.
- **Phenomenology of Compression:** The list of consciousness features
  (novelty salience, "Aha!" moments, etc.) ties well to the structural
  view[\[12\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=1.%20,unconscious).
  It might help to note that these are consistent with psychological
  findings (e.g. surprise and learning signals). Possibly cite Laukkonen
  et al. (2025) here or in 4.2.
- **4.2 Hierarchical Compression:** The "three levels" (first-order,
  second-order, third-order) map onto notions of meta-cognition. This is
  plausible, but emphasize evidence (or lack thereof) carefully. For
  instance, "only humans... achieve third-order" should reference
  literature on metacognition (perhaps cite some cognitive science work,
  like one of Flavell's levels of self-awareness). The idea of
  meta-blankets is interesting; ensure the Parr & Friston (2025) model
  is properly introduced. The text should stress that *this is a model*,
  not a proven neural fact.
- **4.3 Emotions:** Classifying emotions by free-energy gradients is
  creative. The claim *"Emotions are not irrational disruptions but
  dashboard readings"* is metaphorical. It would strengthen the paper to
  cite Solms (2019) or similar, which link affect to free energy, to
  show this view has support. The list of emotions by free energy level
  is clear; perhaps ensure one or two references (e.g. to Friston's or
  others' work on affect). The conclusion sentence is good: emotions
  motivate revision to reduce brittleness.
- **4.4 Hard Problem:** The text sensibly *"addresses (not solves)"* the
  hard problem. This section is careful not to overclaim, which fits the
  author's requested tone. It may be dense, so consider whether all
  points are necessary or if some can be trimmed or integrated. For
  example, paragraphs on alternative approaches (IIT vs FEP) could be
  shortened. It suffices to say *"Our approach is function-first, unlike
  IIT's phenomenology-first
  approach[\[13\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=This%20framework%20can%20be%20contrasted,structural%20pattern).
  Both may be needed for a complete account."* The itemized list of
  "What This Doesn't Explain" is very helpful; keep it to underline
  honesty about limits.
- **4.5 Agency and Free Will:** The compatibilist stance
  (internally-generated variation within constraints) is reasonable.
  Stress again that this is *compatibilist* and causal, not mystical.
  The bullet list of metabolic/behavioral/cognitive agency is fine.
  Possibly mention related literature on free will and predictive
  processing (though that may be beyond scope). The text correctly
  avoids "libertarian" free will. In the last paragraph (variation
  engine), the phrasing is strong; perhaps re-emphasize that novelty
  arises from systematic recombination under constraints, which is a
  kind of agency but still fully causal.

## 5. Logic and Mathematics as Compression {#logic-and-mathematics-as-compression}

- **5.1 Logic:** The argument that basic logical laws
  (non-contradiction, transitivity) are *functionally necessary* for any
  system that compresses information is intriguing. It resembles a
  transcendental argument (as labeled). To ensure philosophical rigor,
  cite or compare with similar claims in logic or epistemology (e.g.
  issues of rational inference). Even a brief mention of a philosopher
  like Lewis on credence, or a cognitive scientist like Oaksford on
  rational reasoning, could show precedent. Otherwise, the argument is
  plausible: inconsistency or broken chains would ruin any model. The
  language *"Logic is the operating system of selection
  itself"*[\[14\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Any%20system%20that%20learns%20,operating%20system%20of%20selection%20itself)
  could be toned down to something like "logic underlies the capacity
  for coherent belief revision" to avoid hyperbole.
- **5.2 Mathematics:** The core claim---that mathematical structures are
  optimal compressions of real constraint patterns---should be related
  to philosophical views (they mention ontic structural
  realism[\[15\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=mathematical%20structures%20are%20optimal%20compression,circumference%20to%20diameter%20in)).
  This is a novel position; ensure to acknowledge alternative views
  (e.g. nominalism, Platonism). The text already contrasts with
  Platonism, which is good. Perhaps add a citation to Ladyman &
  Ross (2007) since they are in references. For clarity: state that
  mathematical discovery is treated as finding constraints, not abstract
  entities.
- **Examples:** The Pi, primes, group theory examples are helpful. Make
  sure they are clearly illustrative: e.g., *"The number π is the most
  compressed description of the circle's ratio of circumference to
  diameter."* That's creative but should be flagged as an interpretation
  rather than the only way to view π.
- **5.3 Unreasonable Effectiveness:** The explanation that physics and
  mathematics explore the *same constraint structure* aligns with
  Wigner's puzzle. Cite \[28\] on how general relativity and
  differential geometry converged as an example. It might help to
  mention other literature on this (maybe Tegmark's Mathematical
  Universe Hypothesis is a contrasting view, to differentiate your
  stance). But keep focus: emphasize that independent discovery of
  structures is because constraints "dictate" them, not cosmic
  coincidence. Possibly cite \[27\] where the π analogy is made.

## 9. Implications and Open Questions {#implications-and-open-questions}

- **9.1 Philosophy of Mind:** The summary table points are mostly
  reiterations. Possibly shorten: focus on key insights (phenomenology
  as structural, meta-blanket = self-awareness, etc.). The note on
  institutional minds without consciousness is intriguing; ensure it's
  clear this is a speculative, contingent claim (institutions process
  information but do not have rapid, bound experience like brains do).
  The citation to James (1890) is apt.
- **9.2 Epistemology:** The redefinition of knowledge/truth should be
  clearly contrasted with traditional views. The bullet *"not JTB, but
  optimized compression"* is provocative. Perhaps rephrase: *"Here,
  'knowledge' is understood not as justified true belief, but as
  information compression that minimizes systemic error
  ('brittleness')."* Use caution: many epistemologists will find this
  unfamiliar. Emphasize it aligns with Quine's naturalism (beliefs form
  a web, no pre-set
  fundamentals)[\[19\]](file://file_0000000098c071f5b9ff508da0bece8d#:~:text=Quine%20argued%20that%20truth%20is,that%2C%20once%20discovered%2C%20resist%20further).

- **9.3 Metaphysics:** The phrases "ontology enacted, not discovered"
  capture the paper's view. Reiterate that this is *compatibilist

  with successful compression are 'real' for us. Avoid implying pure
  relativism. The text already does a good job.
- **9.4 Ethics:** This section is the most likely to attract

  existing Platonic entities. For example, π compresses the infinite

  consequence of underlying

  necessity of the constraints it encodes, echoing ontic structural

  *Original:* "By suppressing dissent...the system goes blind to

  bit of error is a thermodynamic
