### 2. The Decompression Ratio

In academic writing, you state a thesis, provide a citation, and move on.
In trade writing, you **Hook**, **Narrate**, and **Then Explain**.

**Example from your draft (Metaphysics):**
> *Current Text (50 words):* "Mereological nihilism is obsolete. A hurricane is a real object because it achieves computational closure. An $\epsilon$-machine tracking pressure gradients predicts the storm's path better than tracking individual molecules."

> *Trade Expansion (300+ words):*
> 1.  **The Hook:** Start with a specific hurricane (e.g., Katrina). Describe the chaos on the ground.
> 2.  **The Narrative:** Describe the meteorologist trying to predict the path. If they tried to track every water molecule (the "Nihilist" view), the computer would crash.
> 3.  **The Reveal:** Instead, they track pressure and temperature. They ignore the "real" atoms to see the "emergent" storm.
> 4.  **The Concept:** *Then* you introduce "Computational Closure" as the name for what the meteorologist just did.

**You don't need *new* ideas. You need to tell the *stories* of your existing ideas.**
